How to Use the Image Converter

  1. Select Your Image: Click the Drag & Drop area to select an image file from your computer, or simply drag it onto the designated space.
  2. Choose Output Format: Once your image preview appears, choose your desired output format from the "Convert to:" dropdown menu (e.g., JPG, PNG, WebP).
  3. Adjust Quality (Optional): For formats like JPG and WebP, you can adjust the quality slider to balance file size and visual fidelity. Higher quality means a larger file size.
  4. Convert and Download: Click the Convert Image button. After a moment, the result box will appear with a "Download" button to save your new file and a "Start Over" button to process another image.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this image converter tool free to use?

Yes, absolutely! Our online Image Converter is 100% free to use for everyone. There are no hidden charges, watermarks, or usage limits.

Are my uploaded images secure?

Your privacy and security are our top priority. This tool works entirely within your web browser (client-side). This means your images are never uploaded to our servers. All conversion processes happen locally on your own computer, ensuring your files remain completely private.

What image formats can I convert from and to?

You can upload most common image formats like JPG, PNG, GIF, BMP, WebP, SVG, and more. You can convert them to JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP.

What's the difference between JPG, PNG, and WebP?
  • JPG (or JPEG): Best for photographs. It uses "lossy" compression, which means it reduces file size by discarding some image data, but it's great for balancing quality and size.
  • PNG: Best for graphics with sharp lines, text, or transparency (like logos). It uses "lossless" compression, preserving all image data, which can result in larger file sizes but perfect quality.
  • WebP: A modern format developed by Google. It provides excellent "lossy" and "lossless" compression, often creating smaller files than both JPG and PNG at comparable quality. It also supports transparency. It's a great all-around choice for the web.
Is there a limit on the file size I can upload?

Since the processing is done on your computer, the limit is determined by your browser and your device's memory. For most modern computers, converting images up to 20-30 MB should not be an issue. However, for the best experience, we recommend using images under 10 MB.
