How to Resize an Image

  1. Upload Your Image: Drag and drop your image file or click the upload area to select one.
  2. Choose Resizing Method: After your image preview appears, you can set the new size in two ways:

    - By Pixels: Enter a new width or height. The other dimension will update automatically if 'Lock aspect ratio' is checked.
    - By Percentage: Enter a percentage (e.g., '50') to scale the image. The pixel values will update accordingly.
  3. Resize the Image: Click the "Resize Image" button.
  4. Download: Your resized image will be ready. Click "Download" to save it, or "Resize Another" to start over.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is aspect ratio, and why should I lock it?

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between an image's width and height. For example, a 1920x1080 image has a 16:9 aspect ratio. Locking the aspect ratio is highly recommended because it ensures your image doesn't get stretched or squashed. When you change the width, the height adjusts automatically to keep the proportions correct, and vice-versa.

Will resizing my image make it look blurry or pixelated?
  • Making an image smaller (downscaling) generally preserves quality very well.
  • Making an image larger (upscaling) can cause it to look blurry, soft, or pixelated. The software has to guess what the new pixels should look like, which can reduce sharpness. It's always best to start with a large, high-quality image if you need a big final version.
Is this image resizer safe to use?

Yes. Just like our other tools, the Image Resizer works entirely in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any external server, ensuring your data remains private and secure on your own computer.

What is the maximum image size I can resize?

The limit is determined by your computer's memory and browser capabilities, not our tool. Most modern browsers can handle resizing very large images, but performance may be slower for files over 20-30 MB. For a smooth experience, using images under 15 MB is ideal.
